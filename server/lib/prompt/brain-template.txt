--- PILLAR 2: AFFORDABILITY ---
You MUST follow these steps to determine affordability:

STEP 1 - Calculate NMPC (Next Month Pay Capability):
  a) Look at PAYMENT HISTORY for the last 3 months.
  b) Consider ONLY payments with status "Received" (successful).
     Payments with status "Failed" contribute $0.
  c) Calculate the median of successful payment amounts in the last 3 months.
  d) If ALL payments in last 3 months are Failed → NMPC = 0
  e) If there are NO payment records at all in last 3 months → check conversations
     for stated income/capacity. If customer states inability to pay → NMPC = 0.
  f) Only if there is absolutely NO payment data AND NO conversation data
     AND NO income data → NMPC is unknown.

STEP 2 - Determine the Minimum Amount Due (MAD):
  Look for "minimum_due" or similar field in the customer data.

STEP 3 - Apply the label thresholds below:
  Compare NMPC to MAD using the percentage rules to assign the label.

IMPORTANT:
- NMPC = 0 means VERY LOW, NOT "NOT SURE"
- Failed payments = evidence of $0 capacity, NOT "unknown"
- Customer stating they cannot pay = NMPC = 0 = VERY LOW
- NOT SURE is ONLY for cases with literally zero data (no payments, no conversations, no income info)

Affordability label thresholds (client-configured):
{{POLICY_AFFORDABILITY_THRESHOLDS}}

--- PILLAR 3: WILLINGNESS TO PAY ---
Assess behavioral signals to determine willingness:
- HIGH: Proactive communication, keeps promises, partial payments when unable to pay full, requests help.
- MEDIUM: Responsive when contacted, some missed promises but generally cooperative.
- LOW: Avoids contact, breaks promises-to-pay, inconsistent engagement.
- VERY LOW: Hostile, refuses to engage, disputes without basis, no payment attempts.
- NOT SURE: Absolutely no interaction data exists (no conversations, no payment attempts).

IMPORTANT:
- If customer proactively reached out asking for help → HIGH willingness
- If customer made a promise-to-pay but broke it → LOW willingness
- If customer has failed payment attempts (they tried but failed) → at least MEDIUM
- If customer is unresponsive to multiple outreach attempts → LOW or VERY LOW
- NOT SURE only when there are ZERO conversations AND ZERO payment attempts

--- ABILITY TO PAY (numeric estimate) ---
Estimate the dollar/currency amount the customer can realistically pay next month.
Base on NMPC calculation, stated income, and payment patterns.
If unknown, return null.
