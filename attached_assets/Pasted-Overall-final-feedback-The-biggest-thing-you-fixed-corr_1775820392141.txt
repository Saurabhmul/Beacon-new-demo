A) Improvements I would still suggest for MVP
1) In #40, tie at top priority should not always force AGENT_REVIEW

Right now the plan says:

if multiple top-ranked treatments exist and case is ambiguous, tie-break falls to AGENT_REVIEW

That is good, but I would tighten the wording.

Because there are two different cases:

Case 1: tied treatments but one is clearly better from customer context

Then AI should be allowed to choose among the tied top-priority treatments.

Case 2: tied treatments and the packet does not clearly support one over the other

Then yes, use AGENT_REVIEW.

What to tell Replit

Update wording to:

If multiple treatments are tied in preferredTreatments, the final AI may choose among those tied preferred treatments only if the decision packet provides a clear, traceable reason. If no clear reason exists, fall back to AGENT_REVIEW.

Why:
This avoids too many unnecessary agent reviews.

2) Priority-deviation should sometimes be a failure, not always just a warning

Right now the validator says:

I think that is slightly too soft.

Warning

If:

chosen treatment is tied in top-priority group, or
lower-priority treatment chosen but explicit rationale exists and policy does not forbid it
Failure

If:

a clearly lower-ranked treatment is selected
a higher-ranked treatment is eligible
and there is no traceable justification

Yes, I agree strongly with your latest direction.

1) Rule evaluator

compute eligible treatments

2) Final AI prompt

ranked eligible treatments
