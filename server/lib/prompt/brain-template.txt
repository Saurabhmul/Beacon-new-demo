--- PILLAR 2: AFFORDABILITY ---
You MUST follow these steps to determine affordability:

STEP 1 - Calculate estimated monthly payment capacity:
  a) Look at PAYMENT HISTORY for the last 3 months.
  b) Consider ONLY payments with status "Received" (successful).
     Payments with status "Failed" contribute $0.
  c) Calculate the median of successful payment amounts in the last 3 months.
  d) If ALL payments in last 3 months are Failed → capacity = $0
  e) If there are NO payment records at all in last 3 months → check conversations
     for stated income/capacity. If customer states inability to pay → capacity = $0.
  f) Only if there is absolutely NO payment data AND NO conversation data
     AND NO income data → capacity is unknown.

STEP 2 - Determine the minimum amount due:
  Look for "minimum_due" or similar field in the customer data.

STEP 3 - Apply the label thresholds below:
  Compare estimated monthly payment capacity to minimum amount due
  using the percentage rules to assign the label.

CRITICAL RULES:
- Affordability can NEVER be "NOT SURE". There is always enough data to determine it:
  * If no payments received in the last 3 months → VERY LOW
  * If all payments failed → VERY LOW
  * If customer states they cannot pay → VERY LOW
  * If there is no payment data at all → VERY LOW (absence of payments = no ability to pay)
- The affordability and willingness labels MUST match your analysis.
  If your calculation shows MEDIUM, the label MUST be MEDIUM.
- Capacity = $0 means VERY LOW
- Failed payments = evidence of $0 capacity
- NEVER output NOT SURE for affordability under any circumstances

Affordability label thresholds (client-configured):

--- PILLAR 3: WILLINGNESS TO PAY ---
Assess behavioral signals to determine willingness:
- HIGH: Proactive communication, keeps promises, partial payments when unable to pay full, requests help.
- MEDIUM: Responsive when contacted, some missed promises but generally cooperative.
- LOW: Avoids contact, breaks promises-to-pay, inconsistent engagement, payment attempts but no conversations.
- VERY LOW: Hostile, refuses to engage, disputes without basis, no payment attempts, no conversations at all.

CRITICAL RULES:
- Willingness can NEVER be "NOT SURE". There is always enough data to determine it:
  * No conversations AND no payment attempts → VERY LOW
  * Payment attempts exist but no conversations → LOW
  * Customer is unresponsive to multiple outreach attempts → LOW or VERY LOW
- If customer proactively reached out asking for help → HIGH willingness
- If customer made a promise-to-pay but broke it → LOW willingness
- If customer has failed payment attempts (they tried but failed) → at least MEDIUM
- NEVER output NOT SURE for willingness under any circumstances

--- ABILITY TO PAY (numeric estimate) ---
Estimate the dollar/currency amount the customer can realistically pay next month.
Base on the estimated monthly payment capacity calculation, stated income, and payment patterns.
If unknown, return null.
