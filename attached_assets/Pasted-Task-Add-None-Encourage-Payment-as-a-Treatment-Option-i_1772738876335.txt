Task: Add "None — Encourage Payment" as a Treatment Option in Decision Rules
What to do:
In Section E (Decision Rules), the Treatment dropdown currently only shows treatments from Section D (Forbearance, Loan Modification, Reage, Clear Arrears Plan, etc.).
Add one new option at the TOP of that dropdown: None — Encourage Payment
This is a permanent, non-deletable option. It does not come from Section D. It's always available regardless of which treatments the client has enabled. It means "no loan treatment needed, just communicate with the customer."
When "None — Encourage Payment" is selected, show two extra fields in that rule row:
Field 1: Payment Target — dropdown with three options:

At or above MAD (default)
Any amount they can afford
Specific amount (shows a number input if selected)

Field 2: Communication Tone — dropdown with four options:

Supportive — emphasize credit health benefits, encouragement, long-term wellbeing (default)
Firm — emphasize consequences of non-payment, need to engage with us, credit report damage
Urgent — imminent deadline, escalation warning, time-sensitive
Empathetic — acknowledge hardship, offer to discuss options, reassure

These two fields are ONLY visible when the treatment is "None — Encourage Payment." They hide when any other treatment is selected.

Impact on Prompt Compiler:
When the compiler encounters a decision rule with treatment = "None — Encourage Payment", generate this text:
Rule [priority]: IF [conditions]
  THEN: No loan treatment required — encourage payment.
  PAYMENT TARGET: [value from Payment Target dropdown]
  COMMUNICATION TONE: [value from Tone dropdown]
  
  Tone guidelines:
  - If Supportive: Encourage payment, highlight credit health
    benefits, explain how staying current protects their
    financial future. Be warm and motivating.
  - If Firm: Be direct and professional. State that they need
    to engage with us so we can help. Explain that continued
    non-payment will damage their credit report and have
    long-term consequences. Still offer help but create urgency.
  - If Urgent: Emphasize imminent deadline or escalation.
    Clear call to action with specific date.
  - If Empathetic: Acknowledge their situation, express
    willingness to find solutions together, reassure them
    that engaging with us is the first step.
For all other treatments (Forbearance, Loan Modification, etc.), the compiler works exactly as before — no tone field needed because those treatments have their own communication context.
