TASK
Implement treatment priority interpretation in the SOP PDF upload flow only.

This is NOT about customer-level decisioning after treatments are already created.
This task is specifically about the flow where a user uploads an SOP PDF and Beacon generates treatment drafts from that document.

GOAL
When Beacon reads an uploaded SOP PDF and generates treatments, it must also interpret the operational priority of each treatment from the SOP meaning and save that priority in Beacon.

Priority must be interpreted from policy meaning, not from the order treatments appear in the PDF.

BUSINESS LOGIC TO IMPLEMENT

Use this exact priority framework:

1 = Mandatory override
- Statutory, legal, safety, or mandatory handling that supersedes normal treatment choice
- These are not discretionary “best fit” treatments
- Examples:
  - Breathing Space
  - specialist handling for Red vulnerability
  - mandatory pause in collections
  - any treatment/status where the SOP clearly says collections/enforcement must stop or a special pathway is required

2 = Eligibility blocker
- Treatment cannot proceed until a blocker or prerequisite is resolved
- These are gating conditions, not normal treatment choices
- Examples:
  - active insolvency
  - active DCA/litigation blocker
  - missing I&E / affordability information causing hold or review before a structured treatment can be applied
  - any treatment/status that exists mainly to prevent or delay normal treatment selection until required information or conditions are resolved

3 = Structured support
- Ongoing or longer-term hardship treatment
- Used where the SOP describes a sustained support arrangement
- Examples:
  - Payment Plan
  - DMP
  - Interest Freeze
  - token payment arrangement if framed as a structured longer-term support tool

4 = Temporary support
- Short-term arrangement or holding treatment
- Used where the SOP describes a temporary pause, short commitment, or short holding action
- Examples:
  - Promise to Pay
  - Payment Deferral
  - Account Hold

5 = Fallback or review
- Standard collections path, encourage payment, or generic review/manual review where no stronger category applies
- Use this if the SOP treatment does not clearly fit into 1-4
- This is the default fallback

IMPORTANT INTERPRETATION RULES

1. Priority is derived from the meaning of the treatment in the SOP, not the section order in the PDF.
2. Do not assume the first treatment in the document is highest priority.
3. If the SOP describes a treatment as mandatory, statutory, safety-related, or requiring collections suspension, it should usually be Priority 1.
4. If the SOP describes a treatment or status mainly as a blocker, prerequisite, or gate before other treatment can be chosen, it should usually be Priority 2.
5. If the SOP describes a treatment as an ongoing affordability support arrangement, it should usually be Priority 3.
6. If the SOP describes a treatment as short-term, temporary, pending review, or a brief holding state, it should usually be Priority 4.
7. If the treatment is generic, fallback, standard path, or unclear, use Priority 5.
8. When uncertain, choose the closest category and explain the uncertainty in the reason text.
9. Beacon should store both:
   - the numeric priority
   - the interpreted priority basis/category
   - the reason why that priority was assigned
